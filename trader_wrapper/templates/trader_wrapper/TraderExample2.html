To understand how trading works on the platform, consider the following example (and figure below). You receive Z=1000 ECU at the beginning of the round. Cash earns an interest rate of 2% each round, and the borrowing rate is 5%. The current stock prices are 5.15 ECU and 7.20 ECU, for stocks A and B, respectively. Stock A (B) has an expected return of 10% (8%) and a volatility of 20% (15%). In the examples, we consider a zero trading fee (f=0).
Example B: Borrowing
At the beginning of the round, you borrow 379 ECU. With the 1379 ECU (the original plus the borrowed amount), you buy 100 stocks A (worth 100 x 5.15 = 515 ECU) and 120 stocks B (worth 120 x 7.20 = 864 ECU).
You keep these positions until the end of the round. At that point, the price of Stock A is 4.45 ECU (i.e., lower than before) and the price of stock B is 8.05 ECU (i.e., higher than before).
  • Your position in stock A is now worth 100 x 4.45 = 445 ECU: You have lost 70 ECU.
  • Your position in stock B is now worth 120 x 8.05 = 966 ECU: You have gained 102 ECU.
  • You have to pay 18.95 ECU interest on your borrowed amount (5% on 379 ECU).
  • Overall, you earned 13.05 ECU during this round (a return of 1.30%).
  • Your round payoff is 1013.05 ECU.
